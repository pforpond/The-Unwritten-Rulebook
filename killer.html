The Killer Rulebook

The unofficial community "rules" that killers are expected to follow in Dead by Daylight

The Unwritten Rules are more like guidelines than actual rules!

Avoid Camping

"Camping" refers to staying very close to a hooked survivor, preventing other survivors from safely rescuing them. According to the unwritten rulebook, a killer should hook a survivor and then leave the area to allow for potential rescues.

Avoid Tunnelling

"Tunneling" is the practice of focusing exclusively on one survivor until they are eliminated from the game, especially targeting a survivor who has just been unhooked. If possible attempt to spread the hooks equally between survivors.

Avoid Excessive Slugging

"Slugging" refers to downing survivors and leaving them on the ground instead of hooking them, often done to slow down the game or to down multiple survivors at once. If all survivors are down the killer should begin hooking them instead of waiting for them to bleed out.

Consider Letting the Last Survivor Get Away

Many survivors believe that when only one survivor remains, especially if they were not toxic and their team abandoned them, the killer should show mercy by allowing them to escape through the hatch or exit gate.

Avoid Taking Advantage of Bugs

If a survivor is clearly suffering from one of the many bugs Dead by Daylight offers, the killer should consider not taking advantage of this to keep the game fair.

Avoid Being Toxic

After a match, killers are expected to be gracious in both victory and defeat. Phrases like "EZ" or "baby survivors" are considered toxic and unsportsmanlike.

The Unwritten Rules are more like guidelines than actual rules!
